Super Deluxe Stylophone - Help

What is a Stylophone?

The Stylophone is a miniature analog stylus-operated keyboard invented in 1967. It's known for its simple, buzzy, electronic sound. This is a "super deluxe" web-based version with multiple voices and waveform options.

Controls

  • Pitch Slider: Click and drag the large slider to play notes. The pitch changes as you move the stylus (your mouse cursor).
  • Sustain (Spacebar): Hold the spacebar to sustain the last note you played, even after releasing the mouse button or key.
  • Keyboard Control: The first 16 voices can be played with keyboard keys:
    • Bottom Row: Z, X, C, V, B, N, M
    • Home Row: A, S, D, F, G, H, J, K, L
  • Octave Shift (Up/Down Arrows): Use the ‚ñ≤ and ‚ñº arrow keys to shift the octave of the most recently used voice up or down. The selected voice is highlighted with a blue outline.
  • Add Voice: Click the "Add Voice" button to create a new, independent synthesizer voice.
  • Wave: Change the waveform (timbre) of the sound for each voice. Options include standard shapes (Square, Sine) and more complex ones like FM and PWM.
  • Volume: Adjust the volume for each individual voice.
  • Octave +/-: Click the '+' and '-' buttons to change the octave for that specific voice.
  • Snap: When checked, the pitch slider will "snap" to the nearest musical note, making it easier to play in tune.

About
